In the Beginning there was Nothing...

Just kidding! There was the whole rise and fall of the Monomotapa kingdom (aka Great Zimbabwe), the Ndebele and Boer people fleeing from South Africa and settling in the Zimbabwe, creating the three main tribes of Zimbabwe now known as regions: Mashonaland, Matebeleland and Minicaland.

1830s-1890s

European hunters, traders and missionaries explore the region from the south. Included in this group was Cecil John Rhodes.

Fun fact : The Rhodes Scholarship was started by instruction of Cecil John Rhodes will. Although th orginal clauses specified it only being awarded to white males.

1889

Rhodes' British South Afric Company obtains a British mandate to colonise what becomes Southern Rhodesia (Rhodes...Rhodesia, get it? He really liked the sound of him name)

1922

British South Africa Company administration ends, the white minority opts for self government.

1930

Land Appointment Act restricts black acces to land, forcing many into wage labour.

1960s

Nationalist groups - the Zimbawean African People's Union (ZAPU) and the Zimabwe African National Union (ZANU) emerge. Ian Smith of the Rhodesian Front becomes prime minister and tries to persude Britain to grant independence

1972-1979

Guerrilla war against white rule intensifies. Smith yields to pressure for negotiation settlement

1980

Zanu (political party) leader Robert G. Mugabe wins the independence elections. Zimbabwe wins international recognition in April! Independence day is marked 18th April 1980
